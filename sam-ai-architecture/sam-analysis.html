SAM Analysis & Decision Monitoring

Where SAM-AI's output stops being a conversation and becomes an auditable record — valuations get scored against comparables, performance gets tracked over time, and every AI-assisted decision leaves a trail back to what informed it.

Why This Pillar Exists

The first four pillars of SAM-AI exist to produce an answer: a model, wrapped in a consistent architecture, applying firm-configured instructions to firm and team data. This fifth pillar exists to hold that answer accountable. In asset management, an AI-assisted output is only as useful as the firm's ability to explain, defend, and improve on it later — so every output that reaches a portfolio manager or analyst is scored, logged, and made comparable against both prior periods and outside benchmarks.

Comparative Valuation Factoring/Scorecards

Turning a single valuation into a ranked, comparable judgment.

Rather than presenting a valuation in isolation, SAM-AI structures it as a factor-based scorecard — breaking a company or asset down into the individual drivers (growth, quality, leverage, momentum, and other firm-defined factors) that feed into the overall view, and weighting each one consistently across every name it evaluates. This lets an analyst see not just what the valuation is, but why, and lets the same asset be compared apples-to-apples against peers, sector benchmarks, or its own valuation history. Because the factoring methodology is fixed and applied uniformly, scorecards generated weeks or months apart remain directly comparable rather than drifting with whatever prompt or model happened to be used.

In practice: a scorecard is the bridge between a narrative AI response and a number a portfolio committee can act on — every qualitative judgment the model makes is traceable to a specific, weighted factor.

Performance Monitoring

Tracking whether the AI's outputs — and the decisions built on them — are actually holding up.

Performance monitoring at this layer is distinct from the technical performance metrics tracked under SAM Team Data. Here, "performance" means investment performance: did a valuation call, a risk flag, or a recommendation generated by SAM-AI turn out to be accurate once markets moved? The platform tracks outcomes against the original AI-assisted output over time, surfacing where the model's judgment has been reliable and where it has systematically drifted, so both the underlying models and the humans relying on them can recalibrate.

Decision Making Tracking/Analysis

The audit trail connecting a final decision back to the AI output, data, and reasoning that shaped it.

Every material decision that draws on SAM-AI — a buy, sell, hold, or risk override — is logged with the specific outputs, data sources, and instruction configuration that informed it. Decision tracking captures not just the outcome but the path to it: which model answered, which SAM Data or SAM Team Data was retrieved, and which scorecard or performance signal the decision-maker relied on. Analysis of that trail over time reveals patterns — where AI-assisted decisions consistently outperform, where human override adds value, and where the process itself needs adjustment — turning individual decisions into an institutional feedback loop rather than isolated, unexamined judgment calls.

PMS Comparative Data

Benchmarking SAM-AI's output against the portfolio management systems and processes it sits alongside.

SAM-AI does not operate in a vacuum — most firms run it alongside an existing Portfolio Management System (PMS) and its established analytics. PMS comparative data sets AI-generated valuations, scorecards, and recommendations side by side with the equivalent output from the PMS and other reference sources, so discrepancies are visible immediately rather than discovered downstream. This comparison serves two purposes: it validates that SAM-AI's output is consistent with the firm's existing systems of record, and it highlights the specific cases where AI-driven analysis diverges from traditional methods — which is often exactly where the additional insight is most valuable.

Closing the Loop

The full cycle: a foundation model (Pillar 1), directed by firm-configured instructions (Pillar 2), reasons over firm-wide data (Pillar 3) and team-specific knowledge (Pillar 4) to produce an output — which this pillar then scores, tracks, and benchmarks, feeding what it learns back into how the platform's instructions, data, and models are tuned going forward.
