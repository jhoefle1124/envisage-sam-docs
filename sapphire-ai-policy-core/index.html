AI Best Practices for ENVISAGE Research Teams

How we use Claude, Claude Cowork, and other AI tools across Sapphire and SAM research projects — getting maximum value from every token while protecting the people and clients our work depends on.

Why This Policy Exists

ENVISAGE handles some of the most sensitive research in the regions we serve: person-of-interest and network mapping, supply chain and forced-labor investigations, work for UN agencies and development partners, and fund management data through SAM. AI tools make our pipeline dramatically faster — but they also create two risks this policy manages:

  • Data exposure. Anything pasted into an AI tool leaves our control. For our work, a leaked name or source can endanger a person, a partner, or an engagement.
  • Token waste. Paid AI usage is metered. Sloppy habits — re-pasting documents, endless regeneration, using premium tools for trivial tasks — multiply costs across the team.

The same discipline solves both: send the right content, to the right tool, once.

The Data Sensitivity Tiers

Every AI decision starts here. Classify what you're about to share before choosing a tool.

Tier 1 — Public

Information already in the public domain or with no ENVISAGE fingerprint.

  • Published news articles and reports
  • Public-domain harvested data
  • Generic code, SQL, and formatting questions
  • General research methods questions
Tier 2 — Internal

Our work product, with no names, clients, or identifying detail.

  • Anonymized draft analysis
  • Internal methodology and process docs
  • Database schemas (structure only, no rows)
  • Website and marketing copy
Tier 3 — Restricted

Never enters any external AI tool except Claude under the rules on the Data Security page.

  • Client deliverables (UNOPS, LIFT, OCHA, SoCha and all others)
  • Person-of-interest and network mapping data
  • Field staff, source, and partner identities
  • SAM client fund data and decision records
  • Anything from conflict-affected regions naming people or organizations

The Five Golden Rules

1

Classify before you paste

Tier 1, 2, or 3? If you can't decide in five seconds, treat it as Tier 3 and ask your project lead.

2

Never connect email

No AI tool — including Claude — gets connected to ENVISAGE email accounts. Email contains client correspondence, source contacts, and contract details. No exceptions.

3

Minimum necessary access

Cowork gets one project folder, not your Drive. Prompts get the relevant excerpt, not the whole document. Verification tools get the claim, not the report.

4

Client deliverables stay home

Confidential documents and worksheets we create for clients are never uploaded to, shared with, or stored in any external AI service beyond the approved Claude workflow.

5

Route work to the cheapest safe tool

Save Claude tokens for high-value contextual work. Generic Tier 1 tasks go to free tools. See Token Efficiency.
