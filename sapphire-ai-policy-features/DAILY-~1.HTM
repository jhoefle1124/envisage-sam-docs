Daily Price Metrics Tool

Ask SAM for a company's price trend, valuation ratios, or financial fundamentals over any date range, and the tool picks a sensible aggregation, formats the numbers professionally, and caches repeat queries — instead of you pulling and formatting raw daily rows yourself.

What's New

  • Price trend queries: daily closing prices and price movement over any date range.
  • Valuation metrics: PE ratio, price-to-book, EV/EBITDA, and similar ratios tracked over time.
  • Market data: market capitalization, dividend yield, shares outstanding.
  • Financial fundamentals: revenue, EBITDA, core net profit, and related figures.
  • Smart aggregation: the tool suggests weekly or monthly aggregation for longer date ranges instead of dumping raw daily rows.
  • Professional formatting and caching: financial values are formatted consistently, and repeated identical queries are faster thanks to a 24-hour cache.

What You Can Ask

Price trends

  • "Show me Apple's stock price trend for last month."
  • "Track Microsoft's closing prices over the last quarter."

Valuation metrics

  • "How has Amazon's PE ratio changed over the last few months?"
  • "Show me Google's PE ratios and price-to-book values for the last 30 days."

Market data

  • "Show me how Apple's market capitalization changed last quarter."
  • "What were Microsoft's dividend yields over the last two quarters?"

Financial fundamentals

  • "Show me Amazon's revenue trend for the last 60 days."
  • "How has Google's EBITDA performed over the last few months?"

Company identification is flexible — a ticker ("AAPL"), a full name ("Apple Inc."), or mixed case all resolve the same way. Vague requests like "what's been happening with Apple's stock lately?" default to a sensible recent window rather than failing.

Smart Aggregation

Query SpanBehavior
Up to ~60 daysDaily data returned as-is
More than ~60 daysWeekly aggregation suggested
More than ~180 daysMonthly aggregation suggested

Asking for a single field — just the closing price, for example — is more efficient than pulling a full category of metrics when that's all you need. See Token Efficiency for why narrower requests are generally the better habit.

Formatting & Performance

  • Professional formatting: large figures use T/B/M suffixes, percentages are formatted as percentages, and prices display cleanly (e.g. $150, not $150.00, for values at or above 100).
  • Caching: identical queries are cached for 24 hours, so a repeated question comes back faster the second time.

Handling Edge Cases

The tool is built to degrade gracefully rather than error out unhelpfully: a company that doesn't exist, a date range with no data (too far in the past or in the future), or an unusually broad request all return a clear, sensible response instead of a raw failure.

Data Handling Still Applies

Price, valuation, and fundamentals data for a publicly listed company is generally Tier 1 on its own. As with other tools on this site, the tier shifts once results are tied to ENVISAGE or client-specific context.

Usually fine

Standalone public metrics

Pulling price trends or valuation ratios for a publicly listed company, with no client framing, stays close to Tier 1.

Check first

Tied to a portfolio or engagement

If the query or its framing ties these metrics to a specific client portfolio or engagement, treat it as Tier 2/3 and keep it inside SAM and approved workflows, per Data Security.
